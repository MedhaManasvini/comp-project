Women's rights

Movements



Women have long been the driving force behind socio-cultural reform movements in India. It was women who fought for the right to education, who protested against the practice of Sati, and for a ban on child marriage. It was women who struggled against the caste system, for the abolition of slavery

19th century

The first women's rights movement in India focused on education, property rights, and the right to earn a living. Social reformers like Raja Ram Mohan Roy, Ishwar Chandra Vidya Sagar, and MG Ranade brought women's issues to the forefront.

Post-independence

The Indian constitution addressed some issues of equality and empowerment for women, including the franchise, civic rights, affirmative action, maternal health, and child care. However, many issues remained, including violence against women, exploitation, oppression, and discrimination.

Today

The women's movement in India is vibrant and spread across the country. Activists see the movement as a strong and plural force, taking different forms in different parts of the country.
